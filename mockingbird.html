To Kill a Mockingbird

by Margarita Perez

Synopsis:
Set in the Deep South during the 1930s, To Kill a Mockingbird follows young Scout Finch as she witnesses her father, Atticus Finch, defend a Black man falsely accused of a crime. The novel explores themes of racial injustice, moral growth, and empathy through the innocent eyes of a child.
